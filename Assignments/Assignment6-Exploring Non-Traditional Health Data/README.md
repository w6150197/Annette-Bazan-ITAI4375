# Assignment 6: Exploring Non-Traditional Health Data

## Objective
Examine the role of AI, big data, and genetic testing in population health management and personalized medicine, with a focus on ethical, privacy, and fairness considerations.

## What I Did
- Reviewed how AI and big data enable population-level insights, predictive modeling, and proactive healthcare interventions.
- Explored case studies where analytics improved care coordination and reduced unnecessary ER visits.
- Analyzed the integration of genetic testing services (e.g., 23andMe) in preventive medicine and personalized health strategies.
- Investigated ethical concerns, including privacy, potential genetic bias, and regulatory safeguards.

## Weekly Discussion Insights
- AI and big data allow healthcare systems to shift from reactive care to proactive population-level prevention.
- Predictive models can identify high-risk individuals for early intervention, improving quality of care.
- Key privacy frameworks include ISO/IEC 27701, European Health Data Space regulation, and U.S. proposals like the Genomic Data Protection Act.
- Fairness in AI models is evaluated using metrics like equal opportunity, statistical parity, and predictive equity, alongside human-in-the-loop validation.
- Genetic testing can enhance precision medicine, early disease detection, and pharmacogenomics.
- Ethical safeguards are critical to prevent misuse of sensitive genetic data and ensure proper interpretation.

## Assignment Summary
- **Population Health Analytics:** Aggregates data from EHRs, social determinants, and community-level sources to improve outcomes.
- **Big Data Use Case:** Program serving ~4 million older adults demonstrated reduced ER visits via analytics-guided care coordination.
- **Genetic Testing:** Direct-to-consumer services can identify disease risks, carrier status, ancestry, and pharmacogenomic information.
- **Challenges:** Privacy, security, potential bias, and misinterpretation of results without proper counseling.
- **Importance:** These non-traditional data sources support predictive, personalized, and preventive healthcare, contributing to precision medicine.
