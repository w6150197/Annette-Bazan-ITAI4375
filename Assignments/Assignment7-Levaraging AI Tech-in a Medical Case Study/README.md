## Discussion: AI Bias in Healthcare

This week’s discussion focused on **bias in healthcare AI** and its potential impact on patient outcomes. Key learnings include:

- AI bias can arise at multiple stages of the data and model lifecycle, including:
  - Data collection
  - Dataset composition
  - Algorithm design  
  These biases can produce unequal outcomes for certain patient populations (Celi et al., 2022; Chinta et al., 2024).

- Models trained on homogeneous or non-representative datasets may not generalize to diverse populations, leading to disparities in diagnostic accuracy and treatment outcomes.

- **Strategies to mitigate bias** include:
  - Continuous monitoring of AI systems
  - Updating datasets to ensure diversity
  - Interdisciplinary oversight teams
  - Fairness-aware algorithm design
  These approaches help ensure AI remains fair in real clinical environments without disrupting patient care or increasing costs (HealthIT.gov, 2025; JMIR, 2023; Yang et al., 2024).

## Case Study: 10-Month-Old Infant with HLH

The case study analyzed a 10-month-old infant presenting with severe complications including:

- **Immune dysfunction**: Overactive immune response damaging healthy tissue
- **Neurological damage**: Necrotizing encephalopathy leading to brain tissue destruction
- **Blood cell depletion (cytopenia)**: Low red and white blood cell counts, increasing infection risk

Despite treatment, the infant’s condition worsened, ultimately resulting in death (Arai, Takahashi, & Inayama, 2006).

**AI Applications in HLH Management:**

- AI-powered diagnostics can analyze lab results and symptoms to detect HLH early.
- Machine learning models can predict disease progression and guide treatment decisions.
- AI-assisted imaging analysis can identify brain abnormalities sooner, potentially preventing severe neurological damage.
- Comparison of historical and recent cases (Niizato et al., 2022; Esteva et al., 2021) demonstrates how AI and modern therapeutics improve survival and clinical outcomes.

## Key Takeaways

- Ethical AI design and continuous monitoring are critical to reduce healthcare disparities.
- Early risk identification and AI-assisted personalized interventions can improve outcomes in high-risk pediatric cases.
- Advances in AI and medical therapeutics over the past 14 years show significant improvements in survival and care strategies for HLH patients.
